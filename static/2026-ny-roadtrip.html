Trip Summary

The summer of 2026 gave the Rajpurkar family a road trip to remember — 17 days that arced from a milestone family moment in Orlando, soared over the coastline to Newark, and then swept through some of America's most iconic landscapes before looping home to Jacksonville.

The journey opened on a personal note: Dhanesh's university orientation at UCF, where the family spent four days in Orlando anchored at the sprawling HGV Tuscany Village resort. Blue Springs State Park, Disney Springs, and a proud send-off to campus set the tone — equal parts family milestone and holiday.

New York City was the centrepiece. Four days of relentless exploration — the vertiginous Summit One Vanderbilt observation deck, a night at Broadway watching Harry Potter and the Cursed Child, morning jogs around the full Central Park loop, the solemn World Trade Center Memorial, the Intrepid Museum, the United Nations, Brooklyn Bridge on foot, and the non-stop sensory spectacle of Times Square. The family navigated entirely on foot, Uber, and subway — a genuine urban adventure.

The Hudson Valley leg brought a nostalgic detour through Saratoga Springs — revisiting a place from 26 years past. The Saratoga Spa State Park mineral springs, a charming volunteer-run Automobile Museum, and the stillness of Saratoga Lake made this an unexpected highlight.

Niagara Falls delivered one of the trip's most emotional moments: a family reunion with Sudesh Mama and family for a day of mist-drenched walks on Goat Island, the Maid of the Mist boat ride, waffle ice creams at the rainbow bridge, and fireworks lighting up the falls at night. Watching the mist rise above the American Falls at dusk, sitting on a bench with nowhere to be — that's the kind of moment road trips are made for.

Hershey was pure indulgence — a well-earned sweet stop with an immersive Chocolate World factory tour, an unexpected Civic History Museum visit perched on the highest point in the city, and a seriously over-budgeted chocolate haul. The drive south through Raleigh added a full day at the Natural Science Museum, and the final chapter in Savannah closed the loop with riverfront pralines, a free ferry loop, and the quiet satisfaction of one last city explored before the drive home.

Total photo count exceeded 15,436 across 14 logged days — testament to how richly every stretch of tarmac was lived. A trip of firsts, reunions, chocolate, and the kind of long road miles that quietly bind families together.
